Below the Line (vernacular) • Pod kreską (żargon zawodowy) • An informal accounting expression referring either to: 1) the distribution of net profit figure on the income statement. For example, “the below the line distribution of profits was a dividend pay-out of 35%” (see also bottom line). Or, 2) in a different context, the expression refers to the boundary between short and long-term balance sheet accounts. For example, “the below the line effects on working capital came from long term debt issuance (an increase

in working capital) or fixed asset purchases (a decrease).”
