White Elephant (slang) • Biały słoń (slang) • Any large, expensive and gran- diose project or asset that lacks value because it is obsolete, poorly located, inef- ficient, badly planned or not wanted by the market. Any attempt to sell it will often result in a steep asset discount, perhaps down to scrap value. Like the name implies, white elephants are large but unnatural phenomena. Nobody wants to finance one. Word origins: from the Burmese belief that albino elephants are sacred and can’t be used for work. Rather they must be lavished with expensive and time-consuming care.
