Unbanked, The (vernacular) • Nieubankowieni (potoczne) – Individuals (perhaps more accurately households) who do not have a bank account. Typical- ly, they live in rural areas, engage in barter with fellow unbanked and are largely selfsufficient. Their transactions, such as they are, are conducted either via cash exchange, the post office or perhaps via a friend or relative who has an account. Savings are kept, often literally, ‘under the mattress’ (i.e. a safely hidden place). The absence of a banking habit may be due to various reasons: lack of a need (due to poverty or a subsistence lifestyle), more convenient alternatives (e.g. the post office), a lack of trust in banks or grey market activity which shuns any paper trails.
