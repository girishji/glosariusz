Disaster Myopia (vernacular) • Krótkowzroczność (potoczne) – The ten- dency to quickly forget the lessons of a financial crisis and revert to imprudent behaviour once things return to normal. Institutional memories are thus weak- ened and the same mistakes tend to repeated all over again. There is little learn- ing going on. For example, after experiencing the debt-market crash in 1998 investors piled into even riskier securities in the bubble of 2003–2007.
