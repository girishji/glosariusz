Dividend Policy • Polityka dywidend • The firm’s overall approach to distrib- uting its annual profits via dividends. Obviously, there are preliminary consid- erations such as availability of cash and freedom to payout from loan covenants, etc. However, within discretionary limits, there are several approaches that could be adopted:

Financial managers have two main considerations with respect to dividends. First, what will be the effect on share price? Second, what will be the effect on future financing costs? The higher the payout, the lower the internally generated funding available for new projects will be. If projects are attractive, the firm may have to resort to external financing more often and incur higher issuance costs. Most businesses opt for the compromise of a “steady as she goes” approach. Dividends are paid out in varying proportions of profit but in a sufficiently modest amount that a constant dividend can be maintained over the business cycle that gradually increases with sustainable growth.
