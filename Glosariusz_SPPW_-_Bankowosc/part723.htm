The cure for contagion is transparency and disclosure for healthy banks and swift action by lenders of last resort and regulators in the case of sick banks. In the case of large banks so-called systematically important financial institutions, the other remedy is extra capital (see quote below). Word roots: from the Latin contagio for “touch”. See also Lehman Brothers and meltdown.



The most headline-grabbing prescription was for Europe’s banks. More capital, [the IMF leader] argued, was essential to “cutting the chains of contagion” in the euro crisis. With- out it there could easily be “the further spread of economic weakness to core countries, or even a debilitating liquidity crisis”. She called for what would essentially be a European version of America’s policy for its biggest banks in 2008 – a mandatory capital increase using public funds if neces- sary. Those funds could come from the European Financial Stability Facility.

A call to arms, “The Economist”, 28 August 2011
