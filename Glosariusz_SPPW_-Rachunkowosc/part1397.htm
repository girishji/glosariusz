Consolidation (Accounting) • Konsolidacja (w rachunkowości) • The finan- cial statement portrayal of separate legal entities as if they were one economic unit. In particular, in the case of a holding company, the parent company and the subsidiaries are portrayed as if there was a merger into one enterprise. International accounting standards ignore the legalities of separate corporate existence based on the fundamental accounting

principle of “substance over form”: by virtue of the parent’s ability to control its subsidiary, the group is treated as one company. By way of illustration, we show how consolidation of balance sheets look:
