1226 Shotgun Clause‌


Managers noted that when they were evaluated solely on short- term financial performance, they found it difficult to sustain [in- tangible] investments to enhance [long run competitiveness]. Expenditures on such investments are treated as period expenses by the financial accounting model so that cutbacks are an easy way to produce incremental short-term earnings…The adverse consequences will not show up in the short run, and when they do, they may be on somebody else’s“watch”.

R.S. Kaplan & D.P. Norton The Balanced Scorecard, p. 126

Shotgun Clause (vernacular) • Klauzula przymusowego zakupu (żar- gon) • A buy-sell provision used by related parties in a business venture (say

a joint venture or a partnership) that allows one or more parties to exit at a fair price. By contractual agreement, designated holders of ownership stakes have the right to offer their holdings to another designated investor at a specified price.
