Why would the bond be worth less than its contractual face amount payable in the future at its maturity? If prevailing interest rates in the capital markets are 20%, a process of arbitrage will affect its price. Demand for the bond will fall as potential buyers go elsewhere for better returns. At the same time, existing bondholders will switch out of the lower yielding bond by selling. With supply greater than demand, the price drops until the market for the instrument clears at a new price. Thus the price of a bond will vary inversely to its yield, with the degree of change – called bond sensitivity – depending on competing interest rates. Such arbitrage ensures that an investment’s yield continuously reflects com- peting returns in the marketplace. As the bond’s maturity date approaches (the “pull to par”), the yield approaches the interest rate.
