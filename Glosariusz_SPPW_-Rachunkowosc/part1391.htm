Conflict of Interest • Konflikt interesów • A contradictory situation whenever some- body has at least two different reasons for doing something, especially when one of them is not apparent or even hidden. Simply having two masters is best described as a “conflict of loyalty”. As the Bible reminds us, since we can only be fully loyal to one, it is difficult to trust a shared servant’s sincerity or reliability. However, so long as conflicts are openly disclosed and addressed – see Chinese walls – they tend to be at least manageable. In the

auditing profession, the issue of conflict of interest can arise when a firm is supposed to be acting as both an impartial auditor and as a provider of management consulting services. While the two activities are not necessarily in conflict, commercial interests may create an appearance of the potential for conflict.
