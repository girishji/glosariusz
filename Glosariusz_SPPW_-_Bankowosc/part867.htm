Highly Leveraged Transactions (HLT) • Transakcje wysoko lewaro- wane (HLT) – A category of bank lending designed to facilitate inherently aggressive leveraged transactions usually as a temporary measure to provide

a bridge to more permanent financial arrangements. A “classic” HLT would be a leveraged buyout. Initially, debt loads are very high (in order to finance the purchase price). In the long run, such high leverage is unsustainable. However, the borrower typically intends to restructure their balance sheet with asset sales. Additionally, aggressive operational efficiency measures can boost cash flow generation. The subsequent pay-downs (from a combination of sale proceeds or refinancing by banks more comfortable with an enhanced debt service capacity) will serve to reduce leverage. Another source of repayment could be an equity issue. Lenders in HLT focus on the fulfilment of some kind of post-transaction
