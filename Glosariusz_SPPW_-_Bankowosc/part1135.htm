Uninvestable Banks (vernacular) • Trędowate (potoczne) – A contrived and dramatic term denoting how deeply unattractive bank shares can be to eq- uity investors. Normally, any damage to an investment’s inherent value can be matched by a bargain price. Even in the midst of a turnaround companies often raise new capital. In the case of EU banks, their share prices have plummet- ed reflecting a myriad of problems in 2011 including sovereign debt exposure, a possible EU recession and funding shortfalls (see graph below).
