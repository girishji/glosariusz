Cash Tied Up (vernacular) • Stopień płynności aktywów (żargon) • A reference to how current assets, particularly accounts receivable and inventory, hold onto cash until it is released (i.e. “untied”) through the operating cycle. The period of time for the tie up can be measured in day’s sales outstanding (DSO). We might say: “Our cash is tied up in receivables and can be released through better collection procedures”.
