Ledger • Księga • The collection of accounts of a similar type. Traditionally, a ledger (com- ing from the old English “legger” or book) was a set of pages for each account bound into a book (hence, the term “the books”). What makes an accounting ledger distinctive as book is that, first, it is expandable (i.e. new accounts can always be added) and, second, it has a detailed reference system (known as a chart of accounts). In modern times, ledgers are typically a computer file. Ledgers can be classified into various categories:
