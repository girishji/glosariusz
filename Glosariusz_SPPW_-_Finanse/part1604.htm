Since gold generates no yield and is expensive to safekeep, its in- vestment returns tend to be highly speculative based on the market’s fears asop- posed to any fundamental value creation. Another consideration is the status of banking. When bank deposit rates are negative, there is no opportunity cost to switch from cash into gold. An even stronger motive to withdraw cash from banks is when there is a concern about their solvency. Keynes called gold a “bar- baric relic”. Indeed, speculators who bought gold in 1980 at its peak of $2000 (inflation adjusted to 2009 prices) an ounce and sold at its low in 2000 would have lost 70% of their investment (vs. the Dow Jones’ gain of 1,113%). After 2000, gold rose to $1,000+ in 2008 in reaction to the credit crisis, fears of reces- sion, inflation and political turmoil in the Middle-East. Stay tuned. See also gold coins.
