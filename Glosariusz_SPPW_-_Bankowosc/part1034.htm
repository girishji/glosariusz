Restructuring has become the most coveted, most criti- cised, and most profitable activity of investment and com- mercial banks. It has created demand for most of the bank- ing industry’s most important, most challenging, and most competitive activities such as merger-and-acquisition ad- vice, leveraged buy-outs, junk bonds, bridge financing, global finance, mortgage-backed finance, and market-mak- ing in securities that result from the transactions. These ser- vices have displaced the traditional banking and invest- ment-banking activities of deposit taking and lending, and of the buying and selling of plain vanilla stocks and bonds.

The Global Bankers by Roy C. Smith (1990), p. 251
