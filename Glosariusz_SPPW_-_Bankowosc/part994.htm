Private Banking • Bankowość prywatna – A niche, historically associated with Swiss banks (but today less so; see table below), in the retail or consumer mar- ket in which commercial banks and investment banks will specialise in offering premium services (at premium fees) to the very rich or to High Net Worth In- dividual (HNWI). Apart from the potential returns to be earned, banks will be attracted to private banking by the low risk (the rich tend to invest not borrow; services are mostly asset management) and the desire to satisfy particularly in- fluential (e.g. controlling a business relationship) customers. A lot of private banking originates from cross-selling to the owners of a bank’s existing com- mercial borrowers. In North America, private banking has tended to focus on providing a higher quality of traditional banking services whereas the European tendency is to focus on securities trading and asset management. Private bank-

ing will also be carried on by smaller, specialised ‘boutique’ banks and merchant banks.
