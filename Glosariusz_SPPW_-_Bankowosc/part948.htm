Monitoring Function (in Credit) • Monitoring (kredytu) – Keep an “eye on” or watch carefully the behaviour of credit exposure to guard against any de- viation from an expected norm or benchmark, including a breach of a covenant or default. If and when this occurs, pro-active credit control should be initiated to ideally get back on track or at the very least limit any damage that cannot be avoided. The two functions are intimately connected and neither is much good without the other (hence the common phrase “monitoring and control”). To monitor a loan means having certain ways (reports, tests, activities etc.) to con- tinually assess its performance and judge the status of its credit risk. As Bagehot tells us “banking is a watchful trade...” Monitoring is crucial to a bank’s role as a healthy imposer of financial discipline on borrowers, without which it would be a passive money market participant.
