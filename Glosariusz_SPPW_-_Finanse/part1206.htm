Influence

Impact on Option Value

Current price (Ps)

Underlying volatility (σ)

The greater the price volatility, the more valuable the option

Black Swan Events (vernacular) • Czarny łabędź (żargon) • Zdarzenia zaskakujące • A surprise event – named after the discovery of bird in Aus-

tralia that always existed but had never been seen in Europe – that is entirely feasible but contrary to past or limited experience and which has dire conse- quences. The phrase was coined by the “risk guru” Nassim Taleb (author of Fooled by Randomness and The Black Swan) who aptly illustrates it with the turkey’s experiences running up to Thanksgiving. In the face of the Credit Crisis of 2007–2009, Taleb argues that Black Swans are embedded in the “pseudo-sci- ence” of risk management that can be overly reliant on quantitative models while ignoring commercial common sense.
