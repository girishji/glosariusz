CVP is a powerful decision-making tool used in management accounting. Busi- nesses often fail because they do not correctly understand the relationships at work (e.g. incurring up-front fixed costs that cannot be recovered in the market place). Complexities in CVP arise in the choice of time frames. In the short run, most relationships are fixed. Profitability is most sensitive to sales volume. In the long run, CVP relationships are more variable. As well, the nature of the product’s demand can be changed through factors besides price (e.g. design, promotion, packaging).
