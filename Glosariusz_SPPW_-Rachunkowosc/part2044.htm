sion-making (creative break-through ideas excepted) can be boiled down to four distinct phases or modes of thinking (reference: “Rational Manager” model by Kepner-Tregoe). First, we need to get the facts to see what’s going on (“situational appraisal”); armed with relevant information, we next identify either a problem or an opportunity (“problem analysis”); to deal with the problem or opportunity, we next look at the options available (“decision analysis”) and finally, in the spirit of “look before you leap”, we should consider the future implications of committing to a certain course of action (“potential problem analysis”). Each mode requires conscious discipline.
