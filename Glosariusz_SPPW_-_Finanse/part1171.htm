Asset Play (vernacular) • Gra aktywami (żargon) • An investment or take- over of a company primarily for the sake of capturing its undervalued assets, as opposed to its income generating capacity. An asset play often entails a break-up.

Asset Quality • Wysoka jakość aktywów • The qualitative dimension of assets which are quantitatively measured by financial accounting. Depending on their role, “good quality” assets are liquid, productive and impervious to asset im- pairment. Implicit is the notion that accounting estimates (e.g. depreciation, bad debts provisions) may over-state, or under-state, the true value of assets. See also earnings quality.

Asset Redeployment • Realokacja aktywów • Shifting capital from one activ- ity to another by selling old assets and then buying new ones. The connotation is that some major strategic initiative is at work. To be worthwhile, asset deploy- ment benefits must overcome the associated transaction costs (e.g. selling & buying fees) that tend to often make investments a sunk cost.
