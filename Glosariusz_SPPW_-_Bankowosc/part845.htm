Forbearance • Wyrozumiałość – A lender’s patience and clemency for a bor- rower in default. Forbearance is typically offered in the hope that, with a little time, a distressed borrower will soon be able to cure the default by self-help measures or a return of more favourable business conditions. While creditor rights can always be exercised, it may be unwarranted (i.e. unnecessary and/or too expensive) if the problem is self-correcting. At the same time, procrastina- tion when action is needed can be detrimental. Banker’s judgement must be ex- ercised to ensure that forbearance, while possibly a necessary expediency, does not turn out to be to the detriment of the lender’s position. In the meantime, the lender must ensure that their forbearance does not erode their creditor rights by precedent i.e. “we’ll waive this payment but we reserve the right to expect other payments” nor undermine the borrower’s financial discipline. Another impor-

tant consideration is accurate loan classification. Forbearance – which elimi- nates defaults – should not mask the fact that loans may be non-performing and may need loan loss provisions (see quote below). See also recovery workout and standstill.
