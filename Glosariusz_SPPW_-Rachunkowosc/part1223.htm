702 Anglo-Saxon Accounting Tradition‌


Anglo-Saxon Accounting Tradition • Anglosaska tradycja księgowa • An approach to accounting found mostly in English speaking nations which contrasts to a distinctive approach favoured by many continental European countries. Behind each tradition are differences in law (common vs. codified), capital market structure (more equity financing vs. bank debt), income taxation regimes (size of public sector) and the

public accountancy profession (self-regulated vs. licensed). There are also differences in the way accounting standards are promulgated and applied.
