Pay • Płacić • To give money to somebody in exchange for a product or service. In English, we’ll hear several related terms. “Pay day” means when salaries are paid. A “payroll” refers to a list of people to be paid. The term “payoff ” means taking one’s winnings or, in a more sinister mode, the giving of a bribe as in “they gave the official a payoff to get the licence”. The term “pay-up” means it’s time to come clean and fulfil your obligations after shirking them. Word roots: from the Latin noun “pax” = peace and then the Latin verb “pacare” = to ap- pease and then in French “paiement” and English to the notion of pacifying a creditor.
