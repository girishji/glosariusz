Day Traders (vernacular) • Drobni spekulanci (żargon) • Intraday trading conducted by small, retail investors on the internet who will trade small amounts several times a day. While profit-taking on each trade may be quite modest, if enough transactions are consummated, some worthwhile returns (at least in terms of time devoted) can be made. While they are unabashedly acting as spec- ulators, day traders make the market more “democratic”, active and atomistic. This adds to its liquidity and efficiency. They also force market makers, whom they often trade against, to be alert. A concern, however, is that they promote volatility. Admittedly, some of them are simply feeding a gambling addiction as was the case during the dotcom boom’n’bust period of 1998–2001.
