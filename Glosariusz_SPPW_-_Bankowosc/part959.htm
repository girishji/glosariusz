No damned good

No Damned Good (NDG) • Żadnych kontaktów! – A banker’s harsh de- scription of a borrower with flawed character or deficient integrity. Ability to repay is usually what most bankers worry about. For people who are NDG, the requisite willingness to repay is lacking (see won’t pay or can’t pay). Such people are seldom worth dealing with no matter how favourable the circumstances (e.g. attractive interest rates or security) appear to be. Because they are out to fool the banker, their ethos destroys the very foundation of credit: trust. See also confidence (lender’s) and character loan.
