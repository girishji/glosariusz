Risk Appetite (slang) • Apetyt na ryzyko (slang) – The willingness, usually prompted by the associated reward, to accept – as in swallow – risk. The idea of risk-reward trade-off suggests that risk can be offset by returns. Or, risk can be “priced”. A risk-appetite sets the boundaries beyond which no amount of po- tential reward will be tempting. A decision-maker’s risk appetite will vary with a number of factors:

◆ your “mandate” to accept risk

◆ your ability to understand, and quantify, the real risks

◆ how vulnerable you are to the downside of a loss
