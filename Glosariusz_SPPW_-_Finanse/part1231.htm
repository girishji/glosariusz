The excess is in relation to minimum amounts for transactional purposes or economic order quantities. A buffer represents a kind of insurance. Although it costs more to carry any excess, in the event of a shortage, (e.g. a stock-out in the case of inventory or a liquidity crunch in the case of cash), there are benefits (e.g. in the case of cash, avoidance of default). Buffer stocks are prompted by the prudential motive.
