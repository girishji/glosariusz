Margin of Safety • Margines bezpieczeństwa • A subsidiary use of cost-vol- ume-profit analysis that tells managers how much sales volume can fall before the business starts to lose money. Forecasted volumes are then compared to break-even volumes and the amount, relative to forecasted sales, defines a “cushion.” The comfort derived from the cushion depends on the volatility of sales. A margin of safety is useful information in gauging commercial risk.

Margin Squeeze (vernacular) • Minimalizacja marży, „duszenie” marży

(żargon) • A reduction in profit margins that often occurs in competitive mar-

kets. Typically it comes from pressure to reduce prices whenever new entrants begin to take market share. Other sources can be from cost increases that can- not be passed along to buyers. Finally, margins can fall simply be virtue of try- ing to sell more in markets with a high elasticity of demand (i.e. price must fall in order to increase quantities sold).
