Financial Reality (vernacular) • Rzeczywistość finansowa (żargon zawodowy)

• A situational appraisal of a commercial enterprise in terms of its performance as a

competitive player in the market place. Financial reality asks: how did they get where they

are now? What, in fact, are they now? Who is the competition? How do they stack up against the competition? Where are they headed in the future? Financial reality is what financial analysis seeks to understand. This means getting answers to some basic, funda- mental questions about an enterprise:

◆ what assets does it have?

◆ what is their value?

◆ what liabilities does it have?

◆ can it satisfy those liabilities as they fall due?

◆ how profitable was it?

◆ what is its valuation?

◆ what are the likely future prospects?

Financial institutions and capital markets are keenly interested in financial reality. Insights into financial reality come primarily, but not completely, from accounting
