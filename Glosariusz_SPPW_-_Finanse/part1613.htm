Gordon Growth Formula • Gordon Model • Formuła Gordona • A share valuation formulation that explains stock prices (P) in terms of divi- dends (D), the investor’s cost of capital (K) which equals a riskless discount rate under certainty, but under uncertainty could loosely be interpreted as the ex- pected return to the stock) and the expected future growth (g) of the stock’s dividends. The formula is based on the ideas of John Burr Williams after its re- statement by the academics Myron J. Gordon and Eli Shapiro in 1956. Gordon and Shapiro popularized Williams’ original formula by rewriting it as:‌

Breaking apart this expected return into two components, the dividend yield (D/P) and growth (g), translated William’s formula into a language that popu- larized it amongst investment professionals.
