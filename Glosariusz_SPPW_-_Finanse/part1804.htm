MIRR is superior to the regular internal rate of return calculation by virtue of giving a truer picture of the project’s incremental returns (i.e. without assum- ing, as the IRR does, that cash flows are reinvested at the project’s yield). It also solves the “multiple IRR” problem that can occur when cash flows alternate between positive and negative amounts, resulting in two different IRR results.
