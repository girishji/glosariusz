If an item is freely and actively exchanged in an efficient market, personal value will approach market value. However, we need to remember that market price is based on an equilibrium of supply and demand amongst a large group of market participants. Consumers have subjective preferences.

Individuals typically value things – in terms of personal utility – differently than the market. If personal value is less than the market price, the individual will not buy. If personal value is greater than the market price, the individual will buy and enjoy a “consumer surplus”.
