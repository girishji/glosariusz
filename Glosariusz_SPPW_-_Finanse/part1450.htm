We see that duration will always (zero coupon bonds excluded) be less than the term to maturity of an asset or liability. On a time value of money basis, duration measures the period of time required to recover an investment. Any cash flows received prior to duration reflect recovery of an initial outlay; cash flows re- ceived after duration, are profits earned. Duration is a common measure of in- terest rate risk since it is a key determinant of bond sensitivity to interest rate
