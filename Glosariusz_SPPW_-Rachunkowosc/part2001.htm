Small, immaterial and random variances are not worth attention. But if they constitute a cumulative trend over time, even modest variances should be investigated in anticipation that they will become sig- nificant. Finally, attention must be focused on whether the budgets and standards are appropriate in the first place if there is a sustained, one-sided variance. The accountant’s principle of exceptions is part of a broader “management by exceptions” approach to dealing with any situation that is different than expected.
